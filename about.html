About me

Hello! I'm a Cloud/DevOps Engineer with a unique twist—before diving into the world of servers, pipelines, and automation, I spent years mastering the art of legalese. That’s right! I transitioned from corporate law, where I specialized in corporate compliance, dispute resolution, and keeping companies out of regulatory hot water. Now, I build and maintain cloud infrastructures with the same precision I once used to draft bulletproof legal contracts—except now my clients are clusters, containers, and CI/CD pipelines.

While my current toolbox is more YAML than legislation, my background in law gives me an edge. I understand the fine print of contracts, can untangle corporate red tape, and bring a deep sense of accountability and compliance to the tech world. Let’s face it: cloud infrastructure might not come with legal battles, but risk management is still the name of the game. Whether it's adhering to industry regulations or ensuring high availability, my legal experience makes me a natural at balancing innovation with compliance.

When I’m not automating all the things, you’ll likely find me blogging about the cloud or figuring out why Jenkins decided to misbehave (again). Spoiler alert: It’s always the plugins.

In short, I’m a lawyer-turned-cloud-wrangler with a passion for building resilient, scalable systems and a soft spot for efficiency—because if there’s one thing both law and cloud engineering teach you, it’s the importance of doing things right the first time.
